Soil pollution threatens food safety and human health, warns FAO

Date: September 15, 2021

The United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) warns that soil pollution threatens food safety and human health. The FAO urges countries to take action to prevent soil pollution through sustainable agricultural practices.Certainly! The United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) has identified soil pollution as a major threat to global food safety and human health. Soil pollution can result from a range of activities, including industrial activities, the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, and the improper disposal of waste. Soil pollution can affect food safety and security by reducing crop yields, contaminating food crops, and posing a risk to human health. Pollutants in soil can be absorbed by plants, which can then be consumed by animals and humans. This can lead to the accumulation of harmful substances in the human body, which can cause a range of health problems, including respiratory illness, skin irritation, and cancer

Study finds high levels of soil pollution near oil and gas wells in the US

Date: August 26, 2021

A study published in Environmental Science & Technology has found high levels of soil pollution near oil and gas wells in the US. The pollution includes heavy metals and other toxins, posing a risk to human health and the environment.

Polluted soil near Kabul's brick kilns harming children's health

Date: June 9, 2021

A report by Human Rights Watch has found that polluted soil near brick kilns in Kabul, Afghanistan, is harming children's health. The soil is contaminated with lead and other toxic substances, which can cause long-term health problems for those exposed. The report calls for action to address the pollution and protect the health of children and communities living near the brick kilns.

Soil pollution in UK threatens food security, warns report

Date: December 7, 2021

A report by the Soil Association warns that soil pollution in the UK threatens food security. The pollution includes pesticides, heavy metals, and other contaminants, which can reduce crop yields and harm human health. The report calls for urgent action to address soil pollution and protect the country's food supply.

Plastic pollution is contaminating soil worldwide, study finds

Date: October 20, 2021

A study published in Global Change Biology has found that plastic pollution is contaminating soil worldwide. The pollution can harm soil organisms and reduce soil fertility, posing a threat to agriculture and ecosystems. The study calls for measures to reduce plastic pollution and protect soil health.
